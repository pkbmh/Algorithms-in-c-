Thomas Malthus
Although Malthus was a whole hearted disciple of Smith, he was the first economist to analyze the problems of capitalism. Because both his personality and analysis was so dour a journalist dubbed economics "the dismal science" based upon Malthus' work. The three major problems that Malthus tried to understand were poverty, depressions, and over-population.
